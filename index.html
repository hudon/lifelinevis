Tag propagation over time

A tree node represents a process thread. An edge between two nodes represents communication between them. The parent is always the process that sends the tag, and the child is the receiving process. The tree nodes are placed in time buckets based on the time that the tag was sent from parent to child. Clicking on a tree node compresses all the descendants of the node. Hovering over a node highlights all the occurences of the process in the tree. Different threads of the same process are highlighted using a different color. The different colors of the edges indicate propagation of some specific tag (refer to the legend).

Tag propagation graph

A tree node represents a process thread. An edge between two nodes represents communication between them. The nodes can be moved to more convenient locations by clicking and dragging them. The different colors of the edges indicate propagation of some specific tag. Use the checkboxes at the bottom to isolate specific tags.
NOTE: data used for this graph is static at the moment, thus may vary from the tree data.

Thread communication frequency matrix

The processes listed on the left side of the graph are the source processes (send a tag). The processes on the top of the graph are the target processes (recieve a tag). The colored squares in the graph represent communication between the source and the target process. Hovering oher a square highlights the processes involved in the communication. The intensity of the colour represents the total interations (more intence means more interactions).
NOTE: Data used for this graph is static at the moment, thus may vary from the tree data.
NOTE: The 'Order' functionality does not yet have much semantic meaning in examining the data.

Total interations per process

The x-axis contains a list of all the process threads that either sent or recieved tags. The y-axis measures the total number of interactions for the tread. The left stacked bar measures the number of tags sent by the process. The right stacked bar measures the number of tags recieved by the process. Different colors within the bar represent different tags. Clicking on the items in the legend either removes or adds the data item to the graph.
NOTE: Data used for this graph is static at the moment, thus may vary from the tree data.
